Happy Father's Day

Dad, I still remember learning how to drive, In that old blue pickup, barely alive. Coming down Rover Creek Mountain that day, You got a little grumpy, I must say. You huffed, you puffed, you wore your best frown, Like the truck might not make it down. But after breakfast, with coffee in sight, Everything somehow turned out just right. When my buddy Max died and my heart hit the floor, You put work on hold and headed for the store. To Maglio's we went for a Maddax that day, To dig through the frozen ground and lay Max away. Whenever something broke and I didn't have a clue, The first person I'd ask was always you. You always had some kind of off-the-wall trick, half-mad and half-bright. The kind of strange wisdom that turns out just right. Through all the madness that life can bring, The good, the bad, the ugly and everything, Your personality created memories galore, And honestly, I wouldn't change a thing for sure. Late-night coffee, driving winding roads, Interesting stories and tales untold. Hours passed by like minutes do, Some of my favorite talks were shared with you. I'm glad you're around Pop's, more than words can show, And I hope that's something you'll always know. No more grumpy anymore.. you're getting too old. I would rather you get so old you start growing mold. Love you Pop's
— Justin
